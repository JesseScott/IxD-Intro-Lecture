Who does Interaction Design?

Good question.

I would argue that we all contribute to it - designers of all sorts.

Some of it is conscious, some of it unconscious... but the design process is so similar across the subdisciplines, that it's almost always there.

And really, Interaction Design comes from - and owes so much to - other design disciplines - Human Centered Design, Industrial Design, Communication Design...

In fact, if you really get granular, Interaction Design isn't much more than the sum of several different disciplines itself...

So the people that work in IxD come from very diverse backgrounds!

Motivating Factors

CHALLENGE

provide optimal level of mastery that allows actors to directly affect experience

give clear and direct feedback in order to measure progress


CURIOSITY

interaction should be neither too complex or too trivial

utilize cognitive curiosity to help actors anticipate changes and effects


COLLABORATION

create the possibility for actors to influence interaction of other actors

set emerging goals and processes that require multiple actors in order to be successful









Final Thoughts

Interfaces drive the interaction - but also delimit what we can do - a UI helps us avoid errors, at the same time as imbuing a sense of the (im)possible.

What is the Primary task, and what is the Secondary? Likewise, what are the Regular versus Irregular tasks?

Virtually any input can be mapped to any output: a gesture could control a sound, or a light. The same sound could be triggered by something as simple as a button or as complex as a networked event.

Events can be immediate, with feedback, or they can be slow, distanced, or ambient.

Likewise, events can be local or remote, intentional or unknown.
